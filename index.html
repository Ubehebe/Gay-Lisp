Lisp is one of the foundational languages of the computer age. First described in 1960, it has influenced generations of language design down to the present, including "big" languages (Java), dynamic languages popular in web development (JavaScript, Python, Ruby), and advanced functional languages (Haskell, Scala, Clojure).
Scheme is a dialect of Lisp, first described in 1975. It adds several powerful facilities to the core semantics of Lisp, and its formal but succinct specification has made it one of the most widely implemented computer languages.
